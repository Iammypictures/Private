I lost everything — and rebuilt it from the inside out.
Now I help others do the same.

September 2021 · Johannesburg · Rock Bottom

A child looked at me and spoke
a name no record had ever held.

I was a drug addict who had lost everything. My wife. My three children. My self-respect. I was managing a hotel by day — and wanting to disappear by night. I didn't even have a front tooth. I had made a plastic replacement myself. That is how broken I was.

After thirty days of searching for any thread of meaning, a little boy named Conor looked straight at me. I asked the only question I had left:

The question — "Who am I?"
Tedani.
Tshivenda · "Believe me" · "Believe in God"

He answered with complete certainty. No hesitation. No prompting. A word that had virtually no existence in any public record, any database, any AI training set.

Thirty days later I met Conor's parents. They told me about his developmental challenges. I chose not to ask him again — I protected the mystery. In that same moment, he came running down the hallway shouting "Tedani! Tedani!" — and threw his arms around me.

Whether this was synchronicity, grace, or something older than either of those words — it cracked me open. It stripped every excuse and comfortable lie. I stopped trying to manage my broken life. I started architecting a new one.

That is what I now do for others.
